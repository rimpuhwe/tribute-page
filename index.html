Welcome to Rwanda: The Land of a Thousand Hills

About Rwanda

Rwanda, known as the "Land of a Thousand Hills," is a stunningly beautiful country located in East Africa. It is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, vibrant culture, and incredible biodiversity. The capital city, Kigali, is a hub of modernity and a testament to the country's remarkable progress.

TEMBERA U RWANDA Campaign

The "TEMBERA U RWANDA" campaign invites you to explore Rwanda's natural wonders and rich heritage. From the majestic Volcanoes National Park, home to endangered mountain gorillas, to the serene shores of Lake Kivu, Rwanda offers an unforgettable adventure. Discover the warmth and hospitality of the Rwandan people, and immerse yourself in the traditions and stories that make this country unique.

The "Tembera U Rwanda" campaign, launched by the Rwanda Development Board (RDB) in 2016 , aims to promote domestic tourism and encourage Rwandans to explore their own country's rich cultural and natural heritage. The campaign's theme, "Tourism for All," emphasizes that tourism is not just for international visitors but for everyone, including locals.

The campaign has been successful in increasing domestic tourism and fostering a sense of pride and appreciation for Rwanda's natural and cultural treasures. It continues to inspire Rwandans to become tourists in their own country, discovering the beauty and heritage that Rwanda has to offer

Key highlights of Rwanda's Natural Wonders

National parks
  • Volcanoes National Park: Home to the majestic mountain gorillas, as well as beautiful volcanic landscapes.
  • Akagera National Park: Home to the majestic mountain gorillas, as well as beautiful volcanic landscapes.
  • Nyungwe National Park: One of Africa's oldest rainforests, famous for its rich biodiversity, including chimpanzees and the canopy walk.
Lakes
  • Lake Kivu: A stunning freshwater lake perfect for relaxation, water sports, and exploring the surrounding beach towns.
  • Ruhondo and Burera Lakes: Known for their scenic beauty and tranquility, offering breathtaking views of the Virunga Mountains.
Mountains
  • Virunga Mountains: A chain of volcanoes that are a part of the Albertine Rift, ideal for hiking and gorilla trekking.
  • Mount Bisoke: Famous for its stunning crater lake at the summit.
  • Mount Karisimbi: The highest peak in Rwanda, offering challenging but rewarding hikes.
Hotels and Lodges
  • Bisate Lodge: Luxurious eco-friendly lodge near Volcanoes National Park with breathtaking views and exceptional service.
  • Lake Kivu Serena Hotel: A beautiful lakeside retreat offering comfort and stunning views of Lake Kivu.
  • Nyungwe House: Nestled in the heart of Nyungwe Forest, offering luxury and an immersive nature experience.
Cultural Sites
  • Kigali Genocide Memorial: A powerful site commemorating the victims of the 1994 genocide.
  • Iby'Iwacu Cultural Village: Experience Rwanda's cultural heritage through traditional music, dance, and crafts.
  • Ibere rya Bigogwe: a cultural and historical site with beautiful landscapes, making it an attractive destination for both cultural and nature enthusiasts
